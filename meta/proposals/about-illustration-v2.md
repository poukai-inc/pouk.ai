# Proposal: `/about` v2 — founder visual treatment

**Status**: Draft (parked)
**Owner**: Arian (founder) · Author: pouk-ai-pm
**Last updated**: 2026-05-18
**Parked, not active**: this proposal is open but not under decision. No spec depends on its resolution at v1. Status is `Draft (parked)` to signal "exists, not blocking, not actively reviewed."
**Spec reference**: `meta/specs/pages/about.md` §9 (illustration v2 dependency)
**Interview record**: A3 in the `/about` interview on 2026-05-17. The interview surfaced five paths, ruled four of them out, and locked path (iv) — defer illustration to v2.

---

## 1. The decision being parked

`/about` v1 ships type-only — no photograph, no illustration, no signature, no handwritten visual asset (A3 lock). The page's prose carries the operator-introduction job in v1.

The open question parked here: **does `/about` eventually carry a founder visual asset, and if so, in what register?**

The question was *not* "what register?" — it was "should there be one at all, given that the brand has held four pages on pure typography?" The conviction to add a figure was not present at v1; the conviction to add a *specific* figure (kavyasart-register commissioned ink-wash sketch, A3 leading candidate before the re-open) was also not present. (iv) defer was chosen because the decision was not ripe.

This proposal carries the question forward without forcing it.

---

## 2. Why this is parked, not closed

The brand has visually held on typography alone for four pages, which is information — it means the brand is *capable* of holding on typography indefinitely. v1 of `/about` tests whether that holds for the operator-introduction surface too. The decision to add a figure should be made on evidence (prospect feedback, conversion behavior, brand maturity), not on absence-of-evidence (the conviction gap at A3).

The five paths surfaced in the A3 interview, recorded for the future revisit:

- **(i) Type-only.** Currently the v1 state. Reversible at any time.
- **(ii) Mood-board pass.** Curate 5–8 reference illustrators (Lisbon urban-sketch register + adjacent), present as a ranked board with links and fee-ranges. Cheap, reversible. Not in scope until a trigger fires. **Arian declined parallel-track in A3** — do not run mood-board pre-trigger.
- **(iii) Illustrator brief draft.** Author the commission brief (deliverables, register, deadline, fee range, rights) before committing spend. Most useful *after* (ii).
- **(iv) Defer to v2.** Current state. Ship v1 type-only. Re-evaluate when a trigger fires (§3).
- **(v) Handwritten signature / one-line note in Arian's hand.** Scanned, no portrait, no commission required. Rejected at A3 by silence (Arian moved to A4 without picking it up) and earlier at A3's first round (mentioned as Option 5 in the original (b) brainstorm, not chosen). Recorded here as still-on-the-table if conviction shifts.

A sixth path Arian briefly considered and rejected at A3 (Option F-commissioned, kavyasart register, ~$200–600, Lisbon-based urban sketcher) is the one with the most prior thought attached. If a future revisit moves toward commissioned illustration, F is the leading register direction — but it's not the locked answer.

---

## 3. Trigger conditions

This proposal re-opens to active decision when *any* of the following fires:

- **Trigger 1 — first paying engagement closes.** Once pouk.ai has a paid engagement under its belt, the brand's posture shifts from "operator launching" to "operator in market." A founder visual at that point reads as evidence; a founder visual before that point reads as marketing.
- **Trigger 2 — quarterly site review.** At each quarterly site review (cadence TBD by Arian; suggested first review Q3 2026), this proposal is on the agenda. If no other trigger has fired and the prose-only `/about` is still serving the conversion goal, the proposal stays parked.
- **Trigger 3 — explicit prospect feedback.** If two or more inbound emails reference "who am I emailing?" or equivalent (i.e., the prose alone didn't close the operator-introduction gap), the proposal re-opens. One such email is anecdote; two is signal.

If any trigger fires, the next move is the mood-board pass (path (ii) above), then the illustrator brief (path (iii)), then a commit-or-defer call. Path (iv) extends the v1 state through further cycles; path (v) is the cheaper alternative if a portrait-register feels wrong but a hand-evidence-once-removed feels right.

---

## 4. Cross-page implications surfaced at A3

The A3 interview flagged that picking sketchbook-register for `/about` would imply the engraving Pouākai direction on `/` (currently deferred) is either retired or re-rendered in sketchbook register — the site carries *one* illustration vocabulary, not two. Under path (iv), this cross-page pressure collapses to nothing: no illustration on `/about` v1 means no illustration-vocabulary lock-in, which means `/` stays exactly as it is and Pouākai engraving (deferred) stays deferred without secondary pressure.

If this proposal moves to active decision and lands on commissioned illustration, the cross-page implication re-fires: the engraving Pouākai direction must be re-decided at the same time. Recorded here so a future re-spec doesn't miss it.

---

## 5. What this proposal does *not* cover

- Composition (figure size, placement, caption) of any future illustration. That belongs in the `/about` spec revision when this proposal moves to active decision.
- Photographer / illustrator sourcing or fee negotiation. Arian's lane.
- A different content beat for `/about` v2 (e.g., adding a section, reshaping the arc). v2 in this proposal's scope means *adding a visual to the existing v1 page*, not restructuring the page.
- Changes to other pages' visual treatment. The engraving Pouākai cross-page question is flagged in §4 but lives in its own decision space, not this proposal's.
